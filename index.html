Methodology
Data source
Two Wikipedia articles per city: the stadium article (event-specific signal, 2/3 weight) and the city article (destination/economic signal, 1/3 weight). Combined into a single weighted uplift index.
Uplift index
For each article: (views this period ÷ avg weekly baseline) × 100. Combined as (stadium × 2/3) + (city × 1/3). Index of 100 = normal. 500 = 5× spike. Stadium and city uplifts shown separately per row.
Why uplift not raw views
Raw views favour megacities regardless of the tournament. Uplift reveals incremental change — which cities are experiencing the biggest additional attention directly attributable to hosting. Kansas City at 10× tells a more compelling economic story than New York at 2×.
Points system
Cities ranked by uplift index each week. 1st = 10pts, 2nd = 9pts ... 10th = 1pt, 11th–16th = 0pts. "To Date" is the cumulative sum across all completed tournament weeks.
Baseline window
Jun 2025–May 2026 (52 weeks pre-tournament). Stored on first run and reused each week — no repeated API calls. Baselines shown per row as "Normal: X views/wk".
Week windows
W1: Jun 11–17 · W2: Jun 18–24 · W3: Jun 25–Jul 1 · W4: Jul 2–8. Last Week = rolling 7-day window, refreshed every Saturday.
