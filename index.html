Methodology
Three signals
Stadium Wikipedia (50%) — event-specific awareness, cleanest signal.
City Wikivoyage (30%) — travel planning intent, highest economic relevance.
City Wikipedia (20%) — general destination awareness.
Economic Interest index
Weekly views ÷ 12-month average weekly baseline × 100. Expressed as a multiplier: 1.0x = normal, 5.0x = 5× usual attention. Wikivoyage clamped 1.0x–10.0x to smooth noise from smaller sample sizes.
Why uplift not raw views
Raw views favour megacities regardless of the World Cup. Uplift reveals which cities are experiencing the biggest incremental change — the additional global attention directly created by hosting.
Points
Cities ranked by Economic Interest index each week. 1st = 10pts, 2nd = 9pts … 10th = 1pt, 11th–16th = 0pts. First 4 Weeks = cumulative sum of W1–W4 points.
Baseline
Jun 2025 – May 2026 (52 weeks pre-tournament). Fetched once, stored and reused each Saturday. ~ low conf flags baselines below 150 views/week where the multiplier is less reliable.
Week windows
W1: Jun 11–17 · W2: Jun 18–24 · W3: Jun 25–Jul 1 · W4: Jul 2–8.
Last Week = rolling 7-day window, refreshed every Saturday at 08:00 UTC.
