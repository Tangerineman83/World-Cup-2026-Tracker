How much global attention is each World Cup city getting — and what does it mean for their economy?

📋 How we work it out ↗
How to read this table: Each row is a World Cup host city, ranked by how much extra attention it's getting online right now. The Economic Buzz score compares Wikipedia and Wikivoyage views this week against the same week in 2019, 2022 and 2023 — so 3.5× means the city is getting 3.5 times its normal level of global interest. The GDP Impact column shows our estimate of the total extra money the World Cup will bring to each city across the whole tournament — expressed as a percentage of the city's annual economy so you can fairly compare a small city like Guadalajara with a giant like New York. What the columns mean
Matches (played / scheduled)
How many World Cup matches have been played at this venue, and how many are scheduled in total. Updates every night as the tournament progresses.
GDP Impact
Our estimate of the total extra money the World Cup brings to this city — from fans visiting, staying in hotels and spending locally. Shown as a % of the city's annual economy so big and small cities can be compared fairly. Full methodology ↗
Economic Buzz score
This week's Wikipedia and Wikivoyage traffic for this city's stadium and destination pages, compared to the same week in 2019, 2022 and 2023. A score of 4.0× means the city is getting four times its normal level of global interest. Updated every night.
Points
Cities ranked by Economic Buzz each week earn points: 1st = 10 pts, 2nd = 9 pts, down to 10th = 1 pt. Cities ranked 11th–16th score zero. Points update every night.
Why compare to prior years?
Wikipedia traffic goes up every summer anyway. By comparing to the same week in 2019, 2022 and 2023, we remove that seasonal effect and isolate the genuine World Cup impact. Those years were chosen because no other major football events were held in these cities during June–July.
Two signals combined
Stadium Wikipedia (50%) — how often people are searching for the stadium itself. This spikes almost exclusively due to football events.
City Wikivoyage (50%) — how often people are researching the city as a travel destination, which is a strong indicator of visitors planning to spend money there.
