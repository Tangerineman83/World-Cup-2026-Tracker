Methodology
Match columns
Played / Total — official World Cup matches played at this venue out of total scheduled (e.g. 2 / 7). Updates nightly as the tournament progresses. Source: openfootball/worldcup.json (public domain, no API key).
Two signals
Stadium Wikipedia (50%) — event-specific awareness. Spikes almost exclusively due to the World Cup, minimal background noise.
City Wikivoyage (50%) — travel planning intent. People researching a destination to visit — the strongest proxy for economic activity.
Economic Interest index
Views this 7-day window ÷ average views in the same calendar window in 2019, 2022 & 2023. Index of 1.0x = normal. 5.0x = 5× typical interest. Stadium floored at 1.0x (transient fetch failures never collapse rankings). Wikivoyage clamped 1.0x–10.0x.
Why prior-year same period
Compares like-for-like — same calendar window controls for seasonal patterns. 2019, 2022 and 2023 are fully clean years with no major football events in these cities during June–July.
Points
Ranked by Economic Interest index. 1st = 10pts, 2nd = 9pts … 10th = 1pt, 11th–16th = 0pts. Reflects this week's relative performance.
Data refresh
Updated automatically every night at 07:00 UTC (midnight Pacific). Baselines recalculated weekly as the 7-day window shifts, ensuring the comparison always reflects the same seasonal period.
Why uplift not raw views
Raw views favour megacities. Uplift reveals which cities are experiencing the biggest incremental change — additional attention directly attributable to the World Cup.
